Notes on Tracker Usage

Caffeine is modeled in these calculations with an average half-life of 5 hours and a concentration of 120mg per cup.

In healthy adults, caffeine is broken down almost entirely by the liver enzyme CYP1A2 with a typical plasma elimination half-life of 3–5 hours, but individual variation is huge: smokers clear it almost twice as fast, and metabolism is slowed, for example, in people with obesity or liver cirrhosis and users of oral contraceptives.¹
