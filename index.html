Notes on Tracker Usage

Caffeine metabolism is modeled with usual averages. Customize at will (saves a 7-day browser cookie).

Individual half-life: hours Concentration: mg/cup

In healthy adults, caffeine is broken down almost entirely by the liver enzyme CYP1A2 with a typical plasma elimination half-life of 2.5 – 5 hours, but individual variation is huge: smokers clear it almost twice as fast, and metabolism is slowed, for example, in people with obesity or liver cirrhosis and users of oral contraceptives.¹

Caffeine concentration also varies; for example, an espresso can hold ~75mg, cold brew ~190mg, a large drip-coffee mug ~220mg and a Starbucks Venti ~400mg.
